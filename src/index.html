The quarries of Paris, a labyrinthine web of underground tunnels, have their roots in antiquity, when the Romans began quarrying limestone to build the city. Over the centuries, this activity intensified, giving rise to a complex network of quarries stretching beneath the surface of Paris. These galleries, initially created to meet the need for building materials, evolved over time to become a key element in the capital's underground history.

In the 18th century, Paris was faced with the growing problem of overcrowding in its urban cemeteries. Unsanitary conditions and health risks led to the bold decision to move human bones to abandoned quarries. So, under the supervision of the Inspector General of Quarries, Charles-Axel Guillaumot, the transformation of the quarries into catacombs began.

This colossal project involved the meticulous transfer of millions of human remains, creating the famous macabre alignments that can be seen today in the legal parts of the catacombs. However, much of this underground network remains off-limits to the public, hiding little-known rooms and preserving an aura of mystery.

The advent of WebXR has introduced a whole new dimension to catacomb exploration. This immersive technology allows users to bring the hidden rooms home in 3D, augmented reality or virtual reality. WebXR transcends physical limitations, offering a captivating, educational experience that brings to life the history and culture of Paris's catacombs.

In this way, the history of the quarries, the creation of the catacombs and the integration of WebXR converge to offer an enriching experience. This alliance between Paris's underground past, 18th-century architectural ingenuity and modern technology guarantees the preservation and transmission of this unique heritage across generations, inviting everyone to explore the mysterious depths of Parisian history.
